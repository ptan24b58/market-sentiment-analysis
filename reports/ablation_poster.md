## Variance-signal row: explanation

The persona+graph pipeline produces per-event inter-persona variance.
Polarising events tend to produce both high persona variance AND large
absolute abnormal returns. The variance row tests:

    IC( |sentiment_variance|, |AR_1d| )

A positive IC here is a separate, complementary finding: even when the
signed mean signal collapses, the *spread* of persona opinion can still
predict the magnitude of market reaction.

## Two-branch interpretation

The interpretation panel (auto-generated by `src/metrics/interpret.py`)
will display either:

  * **Case A (signal)**: persona + graph IC > nova_zero_shot IC by 2x or
    with significance dominance on both Pearson and Spearman. Headline:
    *"Social graph adds signal."*

  * **Case B (collapse)**: persona + graph IC <= nova_zero_shot IC.
    Headline: *"Personas measurably homogenize."* The accompanying
    variance / bimodality diagnostic explains why.

The pre-mortem ensures the booth narrative is correct in either case.
